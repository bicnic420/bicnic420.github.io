Essay

When people think of New York, they often think about the Statue of Liberty, Manhattan, and the idea of it being the pinnacle of the American Dream, a place where anything is possible. What everyone fails to mention when talking about all the great places and symbols of New York are the trains. Trains in New York are an essential part of everyone's life. Unless you’re wealthy enough to travel by car every day and afford gas, or unless you work from home, everyone uses the trains. The Metropolitan Transportation Authority, or what every normal person calls it, the MTA, is what runs the whole city. No, it's not Dunkin’ Donuts but rather the MTA. At first glance, there's nothing special about a subway system; almost every major city has one, which is why many people overlook it when talking about the grandness of NYC. New Yorkers' lives revolve around trains, whether they like it or not. Most of us would prefer not to rely on these trains every single day of our lives. But we aren’t all blessed with short, walkable trips to our jobs or school. Even then, it's in our nature to seek new things and adventures to go on. This obviously requires going out of your borough because, let's be honest, no matter how much we love our boroughs (or not), we always have to seek more. This will require trains. We, the people of New York, are never content with what we have. We do all our daily responsibilities and don’t just go back home and rest after a busy day at the office. A majority of us will go out somewhere new or old and do whatever we want. We live in the moment; whatever spurts into our mind we will pursue. Like robots, once we get an idea, we stick to it and go seek out whatever it is that we want. Everything moves fast. There's no stopping to think and ponder if our idea is worth it. You would think this makes us dumb and reckless, but it's just our nature; everything is thought of in an instant. We are master improvisers. The idea of a person being what they surround themselves with couldn’t be any truer. New Yorkers move like the trains. We have our goals, our destinations; no matter how short term the joy of our goals may appear to be, they are special to us. The end stops are never ending. Any delays between our goals aren’t enough to deter us. We just take another path; it's all the same in the end if it means we get to our destination. Walking amongst all these goal oriented individuals that I’ve just described would have you thinking that you’ve been living the entirety of your life the wrong way. And to that, I’d say you couldn’t be more wrong. Now I’m not saying that we don’t use our ability to persevere in reaching what we want for good, but our determination isn’t usually used for the betterment of ourselves or others. Sometimes we want really dumb things that would only make sense to us. The greatness about New York, though, is that no one cares how weird or impossible your goal is because everyone is so focused on their own. Their own final stop. There's a sense of tranquility seeing everyone so busy rushing from place to place from their duties to their wants. In a way, you don’t even see them; it's all a blur while you are trying to do what you want. It's a weird experience. You would think that seeing the way everyone moves would be overwhelming, but it isn’t. You shouldn’t feel bad if you're moving about at a slower pace compared to all the experienced people in New York. Hell, some people actually enjoy going at a slower pace, admiring everything they see in the way, taking in the essence of New York with every breath they take. In New York, we’ve got many types of trains; like people, they're all different. Some of these trains go slower than others and stop at more stops, the so-called local trains. Usually they’re emptier, as almost everyone needs the express trains to make it in time to their destination. But as I’ve said before, the trains are what make the city run. Slow or fast, it doesn’t matter. Everyone has their preferences and everyone has different goals, but they all make important contributions to ensure New York is up and running. Whether you prefer the typical fast-paced nature of New York or decide you like to admire every stop on the way to your goals, everyone’s path is connected; every day we interact with people with a completely different approach to living in New York. More often than not,we are so focused on what we want that we barely pay attention to these differences. However different we all may be, we will all reach our final stop because it’s in our nature to eventually get there no matter what delays and changes to our routes there are.
